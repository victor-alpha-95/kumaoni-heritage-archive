Everyday Kumaoni Speech: Greetings & Kinship

रोजमर्रा की कुमाऊंनी बोली
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verificationUpdated 2026-07-12 · review 2027-03

In two lines

A language survives in its ordinary registers: pailāg to your grandmother, ijā for mother, dājyu for elder brother, kas hai ro chha? for "how are you?". Learn ten words from this page and you can address every member of a Kumaoni family correctly — which is where transmission actually starts.

What it is

Kumaoni (~2.0M speakers, 2011 Census) is a Central Pahari language written in Devanagari, classified vulnerable/threatened by UNESCO and Glottolog; Hindi dominates schooling and town life, and youth shift is rapid [V: S1, S2, S3]. It has many dialects — Central Kumaoni (Khasparjiya, around Almora) is the most standard; Soryali, Askoti, Kumaiya and others vary in pronunciation and lexicon [V: S1, S2]. Politeness is grammatical: formal tumar/āpun forms versus informal tu — the verb endings shift with respect [A: S1].

Greetings. The distinctive Kumaoni greeting to elders is पैलाग (pailāg) — literally "I touch your feet"; said by juniors to grandparents, parents, older relatives [V: S1, corroborated by usage across portals]. Formal hello: जै देव (jai dev) [A: S1]. "How are you?" — informal कस हेरे छे? (kas hare chhe?), formal कस हेरो छा? (kas haro chā?); the everyday reply is भल हेरो (bhal hero) — "I'm fine"; bhal = good [A: S1 — Wikipedia phrase table; roman transliterations vary].

Do this at home (today)

  1. Greet one elder properly: call a grandparent and open with "पैलाग" — then ask "कस हेरो छा?" Expect delight.
  2. Swap three words permanently at home: ijā/āmā/bubu instead of mummy/dadi/dada — kinship terms are the lowest-friction, highest-frequency Kumaoni you can adopt.
  3. Answer "how are you" the Kumaoni way this week: "भल हेरो."
  4. Ask the elder what they called their grandmother — dialect variation is data; note it down.

Variations & open questions

  • Transliteration is unstandardized (chhe/cha, hero/haro) — entries keep Devanagari primary.
  • बाज्यू: father (kumauni.in) vs grandfather (mydevbhoomi) — genuine divergence or drift; unresolved [recorded].
  • बुबा (mydevbhoomi: father) vs बुबु (grandfather) — possible confusion between sources; unresolved.
  • Elder sister diju (one source) vs dīdī (four) — outlier recorded.
  • स्वैणि (candidate term for mother-in-law/wife) — not found in any source; do not use until verified.
  • Dialect-tagged phrase lists (Khasparjiya vs Soryali vs Askoti) — absent online → gap register.
  • Upreti 1894 kinship pages (302–313) — confirmed to exist, not yet extracted → gap register (high-value tier-1 upgrade path).
