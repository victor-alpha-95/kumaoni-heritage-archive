The Agricultural Year

खेती-बाड़ी क साल
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verificationUpdated 2026-07-18 · review 2027-07

In two lines

The Kumaoni field-year runs on three seasons — rudi (summer), chaumas (the monsoon that feeds the kharif), hyund (the winter of wheat and barley) — compressed in the proverb ह्यूंद हिमाल, चौमास माल [A/V]. Its grains are the identity crops: madua (finger millet) and maadira (barnyard millet — the Kumaoni name; "jhangora" is Garhwali), bhatt, gahat — sown late May to June by the agricultural university's own advisory, exactly as grandmothers' rules say V.

What it is

The calendar. Native ethnography gives three seasons (rudi/chaumas/hyund); the extension-science framing adds jayad as a third cropping window — a richer local model that popular "kharif/rabi" talk compresses [V/A — both recorded]. Sowing windows from GBPUAT's official advisory: madua and barnyard millet in the second fortnight of May to mid-June (altitude-dependent), amaranth and buckwheat likewise, gahat in early June [V — T1]. Harela's sowing rite sits deliberately at the monsoon hinge of this calendar (see that entry).

The system. Kumaon's mixed cropping and biennial terrace rotation is documented academically as the sari/saar system [V — Springer]. "Baranaja" is a Garhwali term — coined around Tehri's Beej Bachao Andolan (Vijay Jardhari) — and is not the native Kumaoni word; even Kumaon-based sources credit it to Garhwal. Recorded and corrected [V — three sources]. Irrigation runs on guls — gravity-fed contour channels (diversion dam baan; irrigating kulayana; branch channels hawarr, baul) that also drove the gharat watermills [V — T1 via IJTK/CPREEC].

The labor, honestly. Threshing in the courtyard under oxen with work-songs to the bullocks and Bhumiya [A — single rich ethnographic essay]. And the load-bearing fact of the whole system: women carry it — a Bhimtal-block study (n=260) records 12–16-hour days and 75% with lower-back pain; male out-migration has made women "the backbone of the hill economy" [V — T1 + T2]. (A frequently cited hill-women time-use study is from Assam, not Kumaon — excluded [V — negative].)

Do this at home

  • Cook the calendar: madua roti in hyund, bhatt in winter dals — eating seasonally is the agricultural literacy.
  • Ask what your family's sari grew — which terraces, which rotation, whose oxen; one conversation captures a system the web barely knows.
  • Find the gharat: ask if the village mill still turns; photograph it either way.
