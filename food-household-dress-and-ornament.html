Dress & Ornament

पैरण-गैणा
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verificationUpdated 2026-07-12 · review 2027-07

In two lines

The saffron-and-red rangwali pichhoda — the dotted ceremonial veil every married Kumaoni woman wears at weddings and pujas — is the single most distinctive garment of Kumaon, GI-registered since 2023 [V — primary registry]. Around it: the great gold nath, the crescent bulaki, and a wardrobe where honesty requires saying which pieces are Kumaon's own and which belong to all the Uttarakhand hills V.

What it is

The pichhoda. "Rangwali Pichhoda of Kumaon" holds GI Application 874, Class 24, filed 19-04-2022 by a Haldwani women's cooperative, registered 08-11-2023 [V — IP India registry]. Saffron/yellow ground, red dots, central swastika-sun medallion; worn by married women at weddings, janeu, naming ceremonies, festivals A. The folk etymology — pichh (back) + orhna (to cover) — is single-source U.

Ornament. The nath, the large gold nose-ring of the bride, hangs from a chain (sikora) clipped to the hair; the custom of the maternal uncle gifting it is widely repeated but not ethnographically pinned to Kumaon specifically [A/U]. The crescent septum ring bulaki is attested for married women of both Kumaon and Garhwal V; the claim that Kumaonis call it besar is single-source and collides with a Maharashtrian jewelry term — unresolved U. Hansuli (neck torque), pahunchi (bridal wrist), bichhua (toe rings), guluband — all real, and all explicitly documented as shared across Garhwali, Kumaoni, Bhotiya and Jaunsari women: pan-Uttarakhand hill heritage, not Kumaoni exclusives [V — recorded as shared].

Everyday and men's wear. Women's older daily wear is described as ghagra with a kameez/choli and orni — but this rests on thin travel-site sourcing, flagged accordingly U. Men: dhoti/pajama-kurta, waistcoat, and cap or pagri [A — thin]. The "pahadi topi" deserves honesty: the round woolen cap as cold-weather wear is plausibly old, but the decorated Brahmakamal cap now sold everywhere is a documented post-2022 revival product whose popularity followed a Prime Ministerial appearance — the archive records both halves [A — divergence between "traditional" claim and documented revival]. The Bhotiya/Shauka chuba gown is that community's own dress, recorded separately A; a source calling a Bhotiya skirt "pichora" is a probable content-farm error, excluded [V — negative finding].

Where tradition lives now. Weddings (bride in pichhoda; groom in dhoti-kurta, choga, pagri with tikka [A — thin]), Ramleela and chholiya performance — though what chholiya dancers specifically wear is an open gap. The pankhi shawl belongs to Bhotiya weavers (Panchachuli cooperative, Munsiyari), not the generic Kumaoni wardrobe [V — attribution corrected].

Do this at home

  • Find the family pichhoda: most Kumaoni families have one folded away. Photograph it, ask whose wedding it entered the family at, and note the motifs — every one is a data point.
  • Ask about the nath: whose nath does the family keep, who gifted it, at whose wedding was it last worn?
  • Next wedding you attend: notice which of these pieces appear — that's the living inventory.
