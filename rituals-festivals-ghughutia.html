Ghughutia / Uttarayani

घुघुतिया · उत्तरैणी
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verification📅 Updated 2026-07-18 · review 2027-07

In two lines

At Makar Sankranti, Kumaoni children wear garlands of fried jaggery-dough figures — the ghughut — and call the crows at dawn: काले कावा काले, घुघुती माला खा ले — because no one may eat before the crows, who stand for the ancestors A. Bageshwar's ~10-day Uttarayani mela at the Saryu-Gomati sangam is the festival's public face — and the stage of the 1921 Coolie Begar renunciation (see the freedom-movement entry) V.

What it is

The making. Flour kneaded in jaggery syrup, shaped — the "४"-shaped ghughut proper plus damru, talwar, dhaal, tokri — sun-dried, then fried after dark in prescribed silence ("talk, and the ghughute will fly away," children are told) [A — attributed to the late Dr. M.C. Tiwari's account]. Strung with peanuts and seasonal fruit into the mala children wear to bed and offer at dawn A.

The songs. Two overlapping chant variants are recorded (काले कावा काले… / लै कावा लगड़…), converging across two independent sources, plus the daughters'-homecoming song ऐजा कवा ऐजा… उत्तरैणि ऐछा घुगुति ल्यै रैछा A. District rhythm: Almora, Champawat, Nainital and U.S. Nagar make ghughut on Magh 1 and feed crows on Magh 2; across the Ramganga, Pithoragarh and Bageshwar make them the evening before Sankranti [A — named-district detail, single strong source].

The why. Two unreconciled origin legends — the ailing king Ghughutiya, and Chand king Kalyan Chand's kidnapped son saved by loyal crows — both recorded, neither crowned A. Crows as pitra (ancestors) is the through-line A. The Bageshwar Uttarayani mela and its sangam bath anchor the day publicly [V — district/institutional].

Do this at home (14 January 2027)

  • Make ghughut with children the evening before — dough in jaggery syrup, shapes, the frying silence played as a game — string the mala with peanuts and an orange.
  • At dawn, crows first. Sing the call; nobody bites until a crow does. Film it; it is the archive's most photogenic ritual.
  • Ask which side of the Ramganga your family's timing follows.
