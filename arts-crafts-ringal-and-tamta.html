Ringal Basketry & Tamta Copperwork

रिंगाल · टम्टा
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verificationUpdated 2026-07-18 · review 2027-07

In two lines

Two GI-registered crafts hold up the Kumaoni household: ringal dwarf-bamboo weaving (the doka on every back, the soop in every courtyard) and tamta copperwork, the Almora trade that named a whole mohalla "Tamar Nagari" [V — both on the IP India register: "Uttarakhand Ringal Craft" App. 652, "Uttarakhand Tamta Product" App. 653]. Both are down to their last practitioners — ~5 active Tamta families where ~72 worked a generation ago V.

What it is

Ringal. Products: doka (carrying basket), soop/suppa (winnowing), mats and dolls; clusters across Bageshwar, Pithoragarh, Almora (and Garhwal districts — a shared hill craft) [A/V]. Field reporting names the Bajela community of Pithoragarh among hereditary weavers — while this project's earlier "Barudi/Rudia" and "Dwali village" notes were found in no source and are retired [V — premise corrected]. No state minimum price; decline documented [V — PARI].

Tamta. Almora's coppersmiths — gagar, parat, tola, ritual vessels — with the Chand-era origin (craftsmen brought when the capital moved, 1560s) held as legend on top of documented history (the capital shift itself is fact) [A — labeled]. Named living master: Naveen Chandra "Nabboo" Tamta, state "Shilpratan" holder V. The colonial copper-mining-restriction decline story appears in two near-identical tellings — counted as one chain A.

The GI record, corrected. The state-wise IP India register lists both crafts against 27.05.2019 — the same date as Aipan's filing; since Aipan's detail page shows filing 2019/certification 2021, the "registered 2021" vs "2019" divergence is a filing-vs-certification column ambiguity, recorded here rather than silently picked; earlier "GI 2021" phrasing in this archive's hill-life entry is now flagged to this note [A — ambiguity held; detail-page fetch queued].

Do this at home

  • Buy one real doka or soop (they ship; ODOP/GeM lists sellers) — using it is preservation with a handle.
  • The copper audit from the kitchen entry doubles here: an Almora-stamped vessel is a Tamta genealogy record.
  • If in Almora: Tamta mohalla still rings by hand; watch, ask Nabboo-ji's workshop's story, and (with permission) record it.
