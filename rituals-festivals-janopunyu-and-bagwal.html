Janopunyu & the Devidhura Bagwal

जन्यो पुन्यू · बग्वाल
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verification📅 2026-08-28Updated 2026-07-18 · review 2027-07

In two lines

On Shravani Purnima Kumaon does two things at once: households renew the sacred thread — men bathing at the naula while the purohit ties the new janeu — and at Devidhura (Champawat), four clans fight the Bagwal, the ritual battle before Barahi Devi that once demanded one man's blood in stones and now runs on fruit, flowers — and still, in its final minutes, stones [A/V]. It became an official State Fair in 2022 V.

What it is

Janopunyu (जन्यो पुन्यू). The Kumaoni name for the day; men gather at the village naula/spring, bathe, and the purohit performs the janeu change [A — single-source procedure; mantra is the pan-Hindu one, no distinct Kumaoni mantra found]. The local rakhi was traditionally called पौजी (paunji) [A — single source]. Festival foods (red-rice kheer, puri, bade, poowe) rest on one source — recorded, gap kept open U.

The Bagwal. At Barahi Devi temple, Devidhura, four khams — Chamyal, Walik, Gaharwal, Lamgadiya (plus seven thoks) — enter from four directions and pelt each other under bamboo farra shields [V — four independent sources for the khams]. The origin the tradition tells: the goddess once took a human sacrifice by rotation; a grandmother's plea converted it to a stone-battle shedding "one man's blood worth" collectively [A — multiple tellings; Mahabharata and Chand-era layers also attested, recorded as layered]. The modern correction that matters: after a 2013 High Court direction (case papers never located — recorded), fruits and flowers officially replaced stones — but reporting from 2021 and 2025 documents stones still flying in the closing minutes, with 75–150+ treated injuries in recent years (2019 figures conflict: 120+ vs 212 — recorded) [A/V]. Declared a State Fair by the CM in July 2022 [V — two sources + the national utsav.gov.in listing]. Shields post-1945 [A — single source]. "No death has ever occurred" is devotional claim, not verifiable record U.

Boundary note: kheer/lapsi as Bagwal prasad — refuted; the food claims belong to household Janopunyu, and even there thinly [V — negative].

Do this at home (28 August 2026)

  • Renew something: if your family wears the janeu, mark the day properly; if not, adopt the naula-visit — water, gratitude, done.
  • Tell the Bagwal story — grandmother's bargain and all — before the news clips do; then watch one (Resources) with the family.
  • Ask which kham any Champawat-side relatives belong to; kham memory is genealogy.
