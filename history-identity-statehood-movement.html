The Statehood Movement (→ 9 November 2000)

उत्तराखंड राज्य आंदोलन
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verificationUpdated 2026-07-12 · review 2027-07

In two lines

A century of asking — from an 1897 petition to a 105-day foot march — became, in the autumn of 1994, a mass uprising in which hill people, women foremost, took bullets at Khatima, Mussoorie and Rampur Tiraha. Six years later, on 9 November 2000, Uttarakhand became India's 27th state.

What it is

The long asking. The first recorded demand for a separate hill state dates to 1897; milestones follow: the 1938 Congress session at Srinagar (Garhwal) where Nehru favoured the cause; the 1940 Haldwani conference where Badri Datt Pandey demanded special status for the hills; the Fazal Ali commission's 1955 nod toward a separate hill unit [A: S1 — single-thread Wikipedia timeline; treat dates as attested]. The Uttarakhand Kranti Dal (UKD) — founded at Mussoorie in July 1979 (24th or 26th; sources split) by Bipin Chandra Tripathi, Prof. Devi Datt Pant, Indramani Badoni and Kashi Singh Airy — made statehood a standing political demand [A: S1, S3]. Badoni walked 105 days from Tawaghat (Pithoragarh) to Dehradun in 1988; in 1992 he declared Gairsain the notional capital — at Bageshwar, on Makar Sankranti, deliberately in the Uttarayani's shadow [A: S1]. Kumaon and Garhwal carried this movement together; this entry covers both by design.

1994 — the explosion. The trigger was the Mulayam Singh Yadav government's extension of 27% OBC reservation to hill districts where OBCs were under 2% of the population — read as one more decision made in Lucknow for a region it didn't see [V: S1]. Then, in five weeks: Khatima firing, 1 September 1994 (7 or 8 killed — figures diverge) [A: S1, S14]; Mussoorie firing, 2 September (named martyrs including Belmati Chauhan and Hansa Dhanai; a police officer also died) [A: S1, S14]; and the Rampur Tiraha atrocity, night of 1–2 October 1994 at Muzaffarnagar, where police fired on activists bused toward a Delhi rally — six killed, and, per the CBI's own charge-sheet framing versus the police version, women activists were raped and molested in the aftermath [V for the incident and its contested versions: S2; Hindi press corroboration S15]. News of it set the entire hills alight; it remains the movement's defining wound. Convictions came only partially, decades later [V: S2]. Throughout, women were the movement's front rank — a fact every source registers, consistent with who actually populates the hills (see the palayan entry) [A/V: S10, S15].

Statehood. The 1996 Red Fort announcement (Deve Gowda), the 1998 Uttaranchal Bill, and finally the UP Reorganisation Act 2000 — Lok Sabha 1 August, Rajya Sabha 10 August, in force 9 November 2000: thirteen districts, Kumaon and Garhwal both, became Uttaranchal, renamed Uttarakhand in January 2007 [V: S4 (Act text), S5 (Britannica); presidential-assent date shows a 1 Aug/28 Aug divergence in secondary sources — Gazette check pending]. The capital question (Gairsain vs Dehradun) that Badoni answered in 1992 remains, characteristically, still open.

Do this at home

  1. Mark 9 November. Statehood Day is the youngest Kumaoni "festival" — light a diya for it. One sentence to tell: the state was paid for at Khatima, Mussoorie and Rampur Tiraha, mostly by ordinary hill people, many of them women.
  2. Ask your parents where they were in autumn 1994. Anyone from the hills who was an adult then has a memory of those weeks — record it. First-person 1994 testimony is exactly the material the community phase of this archive needs.
  3. If your family holds an andolankari certificate, photograph it and its story.

Variations & open questions

  • Khatima toll: 7 vs 8 [recorded]. Rampur Tiraha night: 1–2 vs 2–3 Oct [internal source inconsistency; recorded].
  • UKD founding date: 24 vs 26 July 1979 [recorded].
  • Presidential assent date for the 2000 Act: 1 Aug vs 28 Aug — verify against Gazette of India → gap register.
  • Sexual-violence record at Rampur Tiraha: documented via CBI framing and press; survivor-testimony scholarship not yet surveyed → gap register (handle with dignity; names never published here).
  • This entry deliberately covers Kumaon+Garhwal jointly; Kumaon-specific movement microhistory (Khatima's Tarai context, Bageshwar declarations) can deepen later.
