Bikhauti & the Syalde Mela

बिखौती · स्याल्दे
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verification📅 Updated 2026-07-18 · review 2027-07

In two lines

Bikhauti is the Kumaoni solar new year (Mesh Sankranti, ~14 April): a bathing day at river confluences to shed the year's "bikh," and at Dwarahat, the Syalde-Bikhauti mela, where three factions — Aal, Garakh, Nauzula — converge on a memorial stone, the Oda, in a rite descended from a real stone-war A. The stone-throwing itself has lapsed; the touching of the Oda (Oda Bhetna) and the jhora-singing remain [A — expert-corroborated correction].

What it is

The day. Vishuvat/Mesh Sankranti, the sun entering Aries — Kumaon's traditional new year, also called बुढ़ त्यार, with regional names Vishtyar (Johar) and Dhaha (Palipachhaun) A. Bathing at sangams with barley-ears or black sesame carries the shedding-of-poison idea A. In Chaudas-Byas (Dharchula), a geographically separate practice has people striking each other with bichhu ghas (nettle) for protection from disease — related in spirit, not in etymology A.

Etymology, honestly. "Bikh (विष) = poison, purged today" is everywhere — and it is folk etymology, never once philologically sourced; a competing derivation (Bikhoti = Vaishakhi, from the solar month) circulates in the same source cluster. Both recorded; neither crowned; and the nettle-link etymology was searched for and not found [A/U — all recorded].

The Dwarahat mela. The legend: two village blocs returning from Sheetla Devi worship fought a bloody battle; the defeated side's fallen chief is memorialized by the Oda stone [A — two independent tellings]. The sequence (per the most precise account): Bikhauti-eve rites at Vibhandeshwar Mahadev; then Baat Pujai by the Nauzula faction; then Oda Bhetna by Aal and Garakh; closing with jhora competitions — roughly a four-day, two-venue affair that the common "three-day fair" framing approximates A. A named cultural historian (Charu Tiwari, in The Tribune) frames Oda-striking as the symbolic residue of an ancient stone-war, kin to Devidhura's Bagwal — with the actual stone-throwing here long lapsed A. The Sheetla Devi temple is dated by the historian Badridatt Pandey to Katyuri king Gurjardev's reign (~12th c.) [A — via named monograph].

Do this at home (14 April 2027)

  • Mark the new year as one: the bath (any running water, a moment of intent), then something sweet — and tell the children this, not January, was the hills' first day.
  • Tell both etymologies — poison and Vaishakhi — and let the family pick; recorded disagreement is the honest tradition.
  • If near Dwarahat in April: the Syalde mela's jhora nights are among the last big open-air song-gatherings in Kumaon.

Variations & open questions

Faction-name spellings (Aal/Garakh/Nauzula vs variants) [A — recorded]. Etymology unresolved U. Exact current form of Oda rite (strike vs touch) varies by report A. Sheetla Devi dating rests on one monograph citation A.
