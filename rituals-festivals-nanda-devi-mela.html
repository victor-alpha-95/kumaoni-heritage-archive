Nanda Devi Mela

नंदा देवी मेला
attestedinternet-sourced, pending community verification📅 2026-09-19Updated 2026-07-18 · review 2027-07

In two lines

Every September, Almora carves its goddesses fresh: banana trees chosen by a rice-grain omen become the twin idols of Nanda and Sunanda, worshipped for five days and then immersed — a Chand-dynasty royal fair that is now Kumaon's biggest religious mela [V — four independent sources for the kadali-idol rite]. It is emphatically not Garhwal's twelve-yearly Nanda Devi Raj Jat — a different event, different district, different century-rhythm V.

What it is

The rite. Rice grains are thrown at candidate banana (kadali) plants; the first leaf to tremble marks the chosen tree — four are taken in procession, and the idols of Nanda-Sunanda are carved overnight, worshipped through the mela, then immersed V. In 2026, Almora's tree-selection procession is reported for 17 September, idol-carving the 18th, with Nandashtami ≈ 19 September [A — on-ground reporting; tithi date pending direct panchang verification].

The history, honestly layered. The fair tradition is credited to the Chand court — Kalyan Chand (16th c.) for the cult's royal adoption; the Almora temple to Dyot/Udhyot Chand (17th c., per the district gazetteer-derived account); Baz Bahadur Chand brought the idol to Almora — 1655 or 1670, sources split, and his reign dates themselves diverge by decades across sources [A — all divergences recorded]. A 2025 local report calls the fair "209 years old" (implying an 1816 founding, after Chand rule ended) — an unresolved contradiction recorded rather than smoothed A. No source supports "1642" — that date, which appeared in this project's early notes, is refuted and retired [V — negative]. Which twin (Nanda or Sunanda) was original is also contested — both versions recorded A.

Where. Almora is the royal, largest celebration (5 days, ~25,000 visitors per the district administration); Nainital and Bageshwar hold their own smaller Nandashtami melas; Johar's villages (Milam, Martoli) keep the goddess's high-valley form [V — two govt sources]. The Garhwal boundary: the Raj Jat is Chamoli's ~280-km, once-in-12-years pilgrimage (2026 is a Raj Jat year); Almora's Nanda joining it is a modern, post-2002 bridge — related, never the same V.

Do this at home (September 2026)

  • Go if you can — Almora's mela week (mid-September) is the single richest live display of Kumaoni devotional culture in the calendar; the idol-carving morning is the heart of it.
  • Retell the omen: rice on the leaf, the tree that answers. Children remember ritual by its strangest detail.
  • Ask your family which Nanda is yours — Almora's, Nainital's, Bageshwar's, or Johar's — and what they remember of the dola.
